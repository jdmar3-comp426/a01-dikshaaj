The Three Main Gods of Hinduism

The three main Gods of Hinduism are Lord Shiva, Lord Brahma, and Lord Vishnu.

  • Lord Brahma is known as the "creator" of the universe, meaning the universe was created by him.
  • Lord Vishnu is known as the "sustainer" of the universe, meaning He is responsible for the upkeep and balance of the universe.
  • Lord Shiva is known as the "destroyer" of the universe, meaning when the universe comes to an end, He will be responsible for its distruction.
