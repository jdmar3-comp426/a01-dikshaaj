The Story of Holi

The Indian mythological tale of Lord Narsimha

In Hinduism, one of the three main Gods that control the universe. Lord Vishnu is known as the "sustainer" of the universe and he has been known to incarnate in different forms on Earth to keep the balance of good and evil. This is the story of his fourth incarnation, Narasimha.

During Lord Vishnu's third incarnation, he killed the demon Hiranyaksha. His brother, Hiranyakashipu, wanted to exact revenge for his brother's death by destroying Lord Vishnu and His followers. He prayed to Lord Brahma, the God of creation, by performing extreme penance. Lord Brahma, impressed, offered Hiranyakashipu anything he wanted.

Hiranyakashipu initially wanted to the boon of not dying, or being immortal, but Lord Brahma said he could not give him immortality, but could give him anything else. Hiranyakashipu asked for a tricky boon - that he would not die on earth or in space; not in fire or water; not at day or night; neither inside or outside of a home; not by a human, animal, or God; neither by inanimate or animate being. Lord Brahma granted him this boon.

With no fear of death, Hiranyakashipu unleashed terror on the Earth. He declared himself as God and ordered people to say no God's name except his. However, this changed when his son, Prahlada was born.

Prahlada grew up and became a devoted follower of Lord Vishnu. Despite the extreme circumstances that his father put him through, Prahlada's devotion and faith continues to grow to be stronger.

One day, Hiranyakashipu challenged Prahlada's belief of God's omnipresence. Hiranyakashipu asked,
"Even in this pillar?", laughing. Prahlada insisted that He was. Hiranyakashipu scoffed, and in anger cut down the pillar.

Suddenly, a half-man half-God, half-human and half-lion emerged from the pillar. At twilight, Lord Narasimha fights Hiranyakashipu on the doorstep and places him on His lap, using his nails to kill him. His death was by neither man nor God, neither human nor animal, not at day or night, not in space or on Earth, not by inanimate object or animate.

The death of the demon king Hiranyakashipu is celebrated as Holi in India every spring.
